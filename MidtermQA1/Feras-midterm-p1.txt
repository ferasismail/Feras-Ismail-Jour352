11. What is the main difference between a serif and a sans-serif font?
Serif fonts have a decorative stroke that hangs off the letters, while san-serifs don't. An example of this would be Times New Roman. San-serif fonts lack the stroke, and are thus smoother in style. An example of one would be Helvetica.
